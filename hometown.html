My Hometown: Surabaya

A resilient metropolis infused with heroism and community spirit.

The Spirit of Heroes

Surabaya earned its title “City of Heroes” from the pivotal Battle of Surabaya in 1945, when citizens united against colonial forces. This spirit still shapes the city’s identity, celebrated every November 10th during Heroes’ Day ceremonies.

Today, Surabaya balances modern development with its storied past. Colonial-era buildings are preserved alongside innovative public spaces, showing how history and progress can thrive together. From bustling markets in Pasar Genteng to the breezy promenades along Taman Bungkul, Surabaya’s neighborhoods showcase the diversity and resilience of its people.

Here are some of the places that represent the spirit and history of my hometown:

Tugu Pahlawan

The towering memorial commemorating the heroes of the 1945 battle, surrounded by a reflective museum.

Taman Bungkul

A lively community hub where people gather for recreation and culture.

Surabaya City Hall

A blend of historical architecture and modern governance, representing the city's growth and organized spirit.

Pasar Turi

A bustling and historic market that has been central to Surabaya's economy for decades.
